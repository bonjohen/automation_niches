Data Privacy & Compliance Automation

Navigate the complex landscape of data privacy regulations with automated compliance workflows. From GDPR to HIPAA to CCPA, maintain continuous compliance and build customer trust while avoiding costly penalties.

Pain Points

  • Multiple overlapping regulations: Businesses must navigate GDPR, HIPAA, CCPA/CPRA, PCI DSS, and SOX simultaneously - each with different requirements, definitions, and penalties that are difficult to track manually
  • Data Subject Access Requests (DSARs): Individuals can request access to, correction of, or deletion of their personal data with strict response deadlines (30 days for GDPR, 45 days for CCPA) - missing these windows triggers regulatory scrutiny
  • Consent management complexity: Tracking who consented to what, when, and for which purposes across websites, apps, and marketing systems creates a sprawling compliance challenge
  • Data inventory blind spots: Many SMBs don't know where all their sensitive data resides - across cloud services, local systems, third-party vendors, and employee devices
  • Breach notification requirements: GDPR requires 72-hour breach notification to authorities; HIPAA requires notifications within 60 days - without automation, meeting these windows is nearly impossible
  • Heavy penalties for non-compliance: GDPR fines can reach 4% of global revenue or â‚¬20M; HIPAA penalties up to $1.5M per violation category annually; CCPA fines of $2,500-$7,500 per intentional violation

Key Regulations We Help You Manage

  • GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation): EU-wide law requiring explicit consent, data minimization, right to erasure, breach notification within 72 hours, and data protection impact assessments for high-risk processing
  • HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act): U.S. law protecting sensitive patient health information (PHI) with strict requirements for access controls, audit trails, and business associate agreements
  • CCPA/CPRA (California Consumer Privacy Act/Privacy Rights Act): Gives California consumers rights to know what data is collected, opt out of sales, delete their data, and access their information with non-discrimination protections
  • PCI DSS (Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard): Mandates security standards for any organization handling credit card data, including encryption, access controls, vulnerability management, and regular security testing
  • SOX (Sarbanes-Oxley Act): Requires public companies to maintain internal controls over financial reporting, including IT controls, access management, change management, and audit trails

Typical Workflow Today

  • Privacy policies are written once and rarely updated - companies discover gaps only during audits or when regulators come knocking
  • Consent is captured across multiple platforms (website forms, CRM, email marketing) with no centralized tracking of who consented to what and when
  • Data subject access requests arrive via email and are manually routed to IT, legal, and various department heads to gather scattered data
  • Data inventories (if they exist) are maintained in static spreadsheets that quickly become outdated as new systems are added
  • Breach response is reactive - when incidents occur, teams scramble to identify affected data, impacted individuals, and notification requirements
  • Vendor data processing agreements are filed in folders without systematic tracking of what data is shared with whom and under what terms

Why It's Hard

  • Rapidly evolving regulations: New state privacy laws (Virginia, Colorado, Connecticut, Utah) and global regulations emerge constantly - keeping policies current requires continuous monitoring
  • Data sprawl across systems: Personal data lives in CRMs, marketing platforms, HR systems, cloud storage, email, and third-party services - no single person knows where it all resides
  • Cross-functional coordination: Data compliance touches legal, IT, marketing, HR, and operations - without a centralized system, balls get dropped between departments
  • Technical complexity: Actually deleting data upon request means finding it across backups, caches, analytics systems, and third-party integrations - manual processes miss data pockets
  • Volume of requests: GDPR alone drove a 72% increase in data access requests in the first year - without automation, each request consumes hours of staff time
  • Audit trail requirements: Regulators want proof of compliance - who accessed what, when consent was given, how requests were handled - manual processes don't capture this systematically

How Automation Helps

  • Centralized consent management: Track all consent across channels in one platform - know exactly who opted in to what, when they consented, and what version of your privacy policy they agreed to
  • Automated DSAR workflows: When a data subject request arrives, the system automatically identifies relevant data sources, compiles information, routes for review, and tracks response deadlines
  • Living data inventory: Continuously discover and catalog personal data across your systems through automated scanning - know where sensitive data lives without manual spreadsheets
  • Breach response playbooks: Pre-configured workflows for incident response - automatically assess scope, identify affected individuals, generate notification templates, and track regulatory filing deadlines
  • Vendor risk monitoring: Track all third-party data processors, their data processing agreements, security certifications, and compliance status in one dashboard
  • Regulatory update alerts: Get notified when relevant regulations change or new laws pass - the system highlights which policies and procedures need updating
  • Automated policy management: Version-controlled privacy policies, cookie notices, and data processing agreements with automatic deployment to websites and distribution tracking
  • Complete audit trail: Every consent, access request, deletion, and policy change is logged with timestamps and responsible parties - generate compliance reports in minutes, not days
  • Risk assessment automation: Conduct and document Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) with guided workflows and automatic risk scoring
  • Training and awareness tracking: Ensure all employees complete required privacy training with automated reminders and completion certificates

Why It Matters for Your Business

  • Avoid devastating penalties: Automated compliance dramatically reduces the risk of fines that can reach millions of dollars - or 4% of global revenue for GDPR violations
  • Build customer trust: In an era of data breaches and privacy concerns, demonstrating strong data stewardship becomes a competitive advantage that customers actively seek
  • Reduce operational burden: What takes days of manual coordination can be accomplished in hours - freeing your team to focus on core business activities
  • Enable business agility: When compliance is automated, expanding to new markets (like the EU) or handling new regulations doesn't require building new manual processes from scratch
  • Protect your reputation: A single data breach or compliance failure can damage brand reputation for years - proactive compliance prevents headlines and customer churn
  • Improve data quality: The discipline of data compliance naturally improves your overall data management, leading to better analytics, cleaner records, and more reliable systems
