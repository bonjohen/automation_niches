Vendor Insurance Certificate (COI) Tracking

Automatically collect, verify, and track certificates of insurance from vendors and subcontractors. Ensure continuous coverage and protect your business from liability exposure.

Pain Points

  • Labor-intensive tracking: Keeping tabs on dozens or hundreds of vendor COIs is highly inefficient when done manually - noting expiration dates, chasing renewals, and verifying coverage details
  • High risk of human error: Relying on human diligence means expired certificates or insufficient coverage can slip through, leaving the company exposed if an incident occurs
  • Fragmented communication: Certificates arrive via email, mail, or fax from different vendors at different times - without a centralized system, some COIs get filed by AP, others by project managers
  • Compliance headaches: Many contracts require specific COIs with proper coverage limits and endorsements - manually verifying every vendor meets requirements is tedious and error-prone
  • Time waste and frustration: Manually reviewing each COI for compliance, chasing vendors for missing certificates, and following up on renewals strains relationships and wastes valuable time

Typical Workflow Today

  • A company requests a COI from each vendor or subcontractor before work begins - the COI arrives via email or mail and is saved in a network folder or file
  • Someone enters key details in a spreadsheet: vendor name, insurance carrier, policy numbers, coverage limits, expiration date
  • That spreadsheet must be checked regularly to see which COIs are nearing expiration - staff manually sort by expiration date and email those vendors a reminder
  • When a COI comes in, an admin manually reviews it against requirements - does it have the required $1M general liability limit? Is the additional insured endorsement included?
  • Certificates are often filed away after review with no alert if coverage changes mid-term - you may not know a vendor cancelled their policy until an incident occurs

Why It's Hard

  • Volume and variability: As a business grows, tracking dozens of expirations across a calendar becomes unmanageable - the more vendors, the higher the chance something slips through
  • Constant monitoring required: Insurance is not "set and forget" - COIs typically expire every year, triggering an annual task for each vendor
  • Detailed scrutiny needed: Not all COIs are equal - manually comparing each certificate to required criteria is a detailed job without insurance expertise
  • Lack of real-time updates: A COI is a snapshot - if a vendor cancels their policy a month after sending you the certificate, you won't know in a manual process
  • Human error and turnover: If the person responsible for tracking leaves or makes mistakes, knowledge might not transfer well and errors compound
  • Legal stakes: If a certificate is mishandled and a claim occurs with the vendor uninsured, the company could face legal liability or uninsured losses

How Automation Helps

  • Centralized COI repository with digital tracking: A single platform where all vendor COIs are stored and organized with key data extracted via OCR - providing a real-time compliance dashboard
  • Automated renewal reminders and alerts: System continuously monitors expiration dates and automatically sends notifications to internal staff and directly to vendors before expiry
  • Real-time monitoring and flagging: Advanced solutions can integrate with insurance verification services or carrier APIs to check if a policy is actually in effect and notify you of cancellations
  • Automated compliance checks: When a new COI comes in, the system automatically compares it against your requirements template and flags deficiencies immediately
  • OCR data extraction: Optical character recognition scans incoming certificates and automatically populates the vendor's insurance record with key information - eliminating manual typing
  • Customizable dashboards and reporting: At-a-glance visuals show compliant vs non-compliant vendors, upcoming expirations, and any gaps - generate monthly compliance reports with one click
  • Secure vendor portal: Many automated systems provide a portal where vendors can directly upload their COIs, triggering automated checks - no more chasing via email
  • Reduced liability and peace of mind: Proactive monitoring and immediate flagging ensure you have up-to-date proof of coverage from all vendors at any given moment
