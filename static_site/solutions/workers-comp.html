Workers' Comp Audit Prep Automation

Automate payroll classification, documentation gathering, and compliance tracking for workers' compensation audits. Avoid unexpected premium bills and disputes with auditors.

Pain Points

  • Cumbersome data gathering: Audits require detailed payroll records by employee and class code, certificates of insurance for contractors, tax documents, and more - all must be manually compiled
  • Misclassification risks: Small businesses often inadvertently misclassify employees or don't update job class codes regularly, leading to unexpected premium bills or disputes
  • Deadline and compliance pressure: Audits follow a fixed schedule and missing deadlines can result in policy cancellation or automatic premium increases
  • Fragmented systems: Payroll data comes from one provider, job roles from HR, subcontractor lists from accounting - manual reconciliation is error-prone
  • Human error and stress: The audit prep process is last-minute and manual, increasing mistakes like omitting a certificate or mis-adding payroll totals

Typical Workflow Today

  • Insurance carrier sends an audit notice after the policy period ends, with a checklist of required documents
  • Payroll reports are pulled from the payroll system (QuickBooks, ADP, Gusto). If the system doesn't break down by workers' comp class, staff manually categorize wages in Excel
  • Staff collect 1099 contractor payments and COIs from all contractors - often realizing during audit prep that some COIs were never obtained
  • They fill out the insurer's audit worksheet, manually typing totals for each class code, listing excluded officers, and providing tax reports
  • Any discrepancies trigger back-and-forth with the auditor, potentially resulting in a surprise premium bill if payroll was underreported

Why It's Hard

  • Data silos and manual reconciliation: Payroll providers might not generate reports in the format auditors need, requiring manual sorting
  • Keeping classifications accurate: Job duties change, employees take on new roles - without regular review, the company might be caught off guard
  • Volume of paperwork: Payroll records, tax filings, certificates - ensuring all records are organized and accessible is a challenge for SMBs without formal document management
  • Technical detail and jargon: Workers' comp audits involve understanding insurance classifications, experience modifiers, and reporting requirements that many SMB owners find confusing
  • Stress and consequences: A botched audit can mean significantly more premium or even losing coverage - the fear of "what will the audit find" is stressful

How Automation Helps

  • Integrated payroll and comp data: Modern payroll platforms can assign each employee a workers' comp class code and track wages accordingly, generating audit-ready reports automatically
  • Real-time updates and alerts: System prompts admins to keep data accurate throughout the year - won't let you run payroll for a new hire unless a comp class code is entered
  • Document management and reminders: Maintain a repository of all necessary documents (COIs, owner exclusion forms, etc.) and set reminders for renewals
  • Automated audit packet generation: Compile all required information into a packet for the auditor with one click, reducing the audit to a non-event
  • Validation rules and anomaly detection: Flag anomalies before the audit - for example, if payroll is 25% higher than estimated or if an employee's classification looks odd
  • Scheduling and compliance calendar: Track policy periods, expected audit windows, and send tasks like "Gather Q1-Q4 payroll reports" automatically
  • Streamlined communication: Pre-fill answers to standard audit questions from HR system data, avoiding duplicate data entry
  • Accurate record-keeping and audit trail: Every payroll run's data is stored and logged with dates, demonstrating due diligence and reducing liability
